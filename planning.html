Planning of the Temple of Heaven

The whole temple covers 2.73 million square miles. The area is mostly square except for the two northern corners which have curved edges. The overall shape of the plot reinforces the idea that heaven is round and earth is square. The outer and inner rings of walls are equal to the shape of the plot.

Environmental Planning

During the planning of a structure, the environment is taking into serious consideration. It was believed that buildings should be synced with the surrounding nature and climate.

City Planning

There were strict regulations and traditions for imperial city planning. The Temple of Heaven was intentionally built in the southern part of Beijing because, in ancient China, south represented the color red, summer, and happiness. These symbolic values were associated with heaven.

Position was symbolically important as was the city organization. The overall shape of imperial cities was square which was, again, a physical representation of a square-based Earth. Cities were surrounded by walls with the imperial places in the center. The roads where all parallel to the north-south and west-east cardinal directions. As a result, imperial cities were a collection of square blocks that made census taking and population control easier. This was necessary during the ancient times, since China had some of biggest cities in the world.
