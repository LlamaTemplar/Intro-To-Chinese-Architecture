Planning of the Temple of Heaven

The whole temple covers 2.73 million square miles.19 The area is mostly square except for the two northern corners which have curved edges. The overall shape of the plot reinforces the idea that heaven is round and earth is square. The outer and inner rings of walls are equal to the shape of the plot.

The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest sits on a three-tier circular marble terrace.20 The temple is inclosed in a rectangular courtyard that consists of three gates, with the main gate at the south. The Circular Terrace in the south is also a marble circular terrace that consists of three levels.21

Feng Shui

Feng Shui is the art of planning and constructing buildings that are in harmony with nature using the knowledge of climatology and geophysics.22 Understanding Feng Shui means understanding how the surrounding environment will affect the interior and exterior of a building. Feng Shui begun in the West Han dynasty (3rd century BCE) and was based on the idea that Earth was alive and had qi (energy). An example of being harmous with the environment would be a building facing south with hills behind it. The hills would provide protection from wind and the front of the house would be heated from the sun.

City Planning

There were strict regulations and traditions for imperial city planning.23 The Temple of Heaven was intentionally built in the southern part of Beijing because, in ancient China, south represented the color red, summer, and happiness. These symbolic values were associated with heaven.

Position was symbolically important as was the city organization. The overall shape of imperial cities was square which was, again, a physical representation of a square-based Earth.24 Cities were surrounded by walls with the imperial place in the center. The roads where all aligned to the north-south or west-east cardinal directions.25 As a result, imperial cities were a collection of square blocks that made census recording and population control possible. This was necessary during the ancient times, since China had some of biggest cities in the world.26
