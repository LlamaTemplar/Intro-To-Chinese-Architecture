Structure

The purpose of Temple of Heaven was to worship the gods.

The Temple of Heaven consists of three groups of buildings. The Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest and the Terrace of Prayer sit in the north. In the south, there is the Circular Terrace and the Imperial Heavenly Vault. To the West are the Hall fasting and the buildings for music and dance performances.

Roofs

In Chinese architecture, roofs are the most impressive feature of a building. Hall of Prayer for Good Harvest has a three-tiered roof of blue glazed tiles. The roof blue color and circular shape is a reference to heaven.

Functionally, the wide roof and overhang shield the interior during the summer but allow the sun to shine inside during the winter when the sun is lower. The roof provides the same functionality against rain water. The roof forms commonly found throughout China are Gabled, Hipped, Half-Hipped/Half-Gabled, pyramidal.The amount of tiers determine the importance of the building. The more tiers, the more important the building is.

Columns

Qinian Dian roof is supported by twelve outer pillars, an addition twelve inner pillars, and lastly, four central pillar.

Columns provide the structural support for the roof rather than the walls. Since columns are visible, the dimensions of the column are relative to the height of the building such that the columns have a good sense of proportion. In an addition, columns are placed in a regular rhythm and divide the walls. Unlike Western architecture, the Chinese make no effort to hide structural elements. In important buildings, the columns are decorated with painted patterns.
