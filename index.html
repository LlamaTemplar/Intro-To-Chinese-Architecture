Ancient Chinese Architecture

Through the Temple of Heaven

Ancient architecture of a civilization can reveal aspects of its early culture. It’s difficult to study early Chinese architecture since most buildings were wooden and perished over time. By looking at the grand evolution of ancient Chinese architecture, one will find hardly any evolution. Until the modern period, the styles and forms of Chinese architecture had remained the same. This resistance to change was enforced by chinese imperial rule and Confucius practice that originated in the Zhou Dynasty (1066 BCE-256 BCE). When analysing the characteristics of ancient Chinese architecture, the details reflect the life and culture of the Chinese. Buildings meet functional needs while conveying meaning through confucius symbolism.


Temple of Heaven

Every year before the winter solstice, emperors of Ming, and later Qing dynasties, would pray to the heavenly gods for rain and good harvest. When the Forbidden City was completed, Beijing became the capital city of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Situated in the south of the city was the Temple of Heaven. The emperor of China was regarded as the Son of Heaven and would interpret the will of the gods. The Temple of Heaven was built so the emperor could pray for the success of crops and that floods or famine won’t occur.
