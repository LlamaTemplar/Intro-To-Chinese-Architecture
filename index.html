Ancient Chinese Architecture

Through the Temple of Heaven

Ancient architecture of a civilization can reveal aspects of its early culture.1 By looking at the grand evolution of ancient Chinese architecture, one will find hardly any evolution. Until the modern period, the styles and forms of Chinese architecture had remained the same. This resistance to change was enforced by chinese imperial rule and Confucius practice that originated in the Zhou Dynasty (1066 BCE-256 BCE).2 When analysing the characteristics of ancient Chinese architecture, the details reflect the life and culture of the Chinese.3 Buildings meet functional needs while conveying meaning through confucius symbolism.


Temple of Heaven

Every year before the winter solstice, emperors of Ming (1368-1644 CE), and later Qing (1644-1912 CE) dynasties, would pray to the heavenly gods for rain and good harvest.4 The Emperor of China was regarded as the Son of Heaven and would interpret the will of the gods.5 The Temple of Heaven was built in 1420 CE in the southern part of Beijing so the emperor could pray for the success of crops.
